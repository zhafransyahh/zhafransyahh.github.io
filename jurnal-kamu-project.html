01 ‚Äî Overview

Project Background

Jurnal Kamu (meaning "Your Journal" in Indonesian) is a mental health application designed to lower the barrier to emotional reflection. Recognizing that mental health care in Indonesia remains underserved, the project set out to create an accessible, non-clinical tool that encourages users to engage with their inner world daily.

The app guides users through a structured journaling experience: selecting their mood, responding to targeted guiding questions, and reviewing a personalized recap ‚Äî making emotional processing approachable, even for those who've never journaled before.

Problem Discovery

Mental health resources in Indonesia are limited and stigmatized. Most existing apps are clinical in tone, creating a barrier for users who want casual, daily check-ins without the weight of a formal therapy context.

üë§

User Research

Interviews and surveys revealed that young Indonesians want a private, judgment-free space to process emotions. They preferred guided prompts over freeform journaling, which felt overwhelming without structure.

Affinity Mapping

Research data was organized through affinity mapping to surface recurring themes: the need for privacy, the appeal of mood tracking, and frustration with apps that feel too clinical or complicated.

üí°

Design Opportunity

A gap exists for a warm, accessible journaling tool that combines mood selection, guiding prompts, and personal recap ‚Äî meeting users at their emotional state rather than asking them to articulate it from scratch.

03 ‚Äî User Flow

How It Works

The core user flow was designed to minimize friction while maximizing emotional value. Users move through a three-step journaling ritual: check in with their mood, respond to contextual prompts, and receive a reflective recap of their entry.
